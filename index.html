Welcome to Cox's Bazaar

The Longest Unbroken Sea Beach in the World

Cox’s Bazaar is world-renowned for having the longest natural sea beach in the world, stretching majestically over 120 kilometers along the southeastern coastline of Bangladesh. This golden strip of sand, washed continuously by the waves of the Bay of Bengal, offers a mesmerizing blend of beauty and tranquility. The beach’s gentle slope and soft, silvery sand make it perfect for long strolls, beach volleyball, and peaceful moments by the shore.

During sunrise and sunset, the sky over Cox’s Bazar transforms into a canvas of crimson, orange, and gold, creating one of the most breathtaking sights in South Asia. Tourists can enjoy the rhythmic roar of the ocean, the salty sea breeze, and the sight of fishing boats dotting the horizon as they head out for the day’s catch. The beachside is lined with cozy resorts, seafood restaurants, and small markets where visitors can experience the warmth and hospitality of the local people. Altogether, Cox’s Bazar isn’t just a beach — it’s a living experience of nature, culture, and calm that leaves an unforgettable mark on every traveler.
